"The Great Gatsby," tells the story of James Gatz, a poor farm boy who successfully transformed himself into a millionaire Jay Gatsby, striving to pursue the youth, rich and beautiful women--Daisy, and he was eventually killed.

Plot

  • In order to get Daisy’s attention again, Gatsby did not hesitate to spend a great deal of money to buy "a magnificent building imitating a city hall in Normandy" on the opposite bank of Daisy’s house. The brightly lit mansion illuminates the night sky every night. People from all walks of life are Come here to revel, here, it seems to have gathered all the glitz of New York in that era And everything Gatsby did is for Daisy. He hopes he can meet Daisy someday in his house.
  • He tried to get close to Daisy by inviting Baker and Nick to his party, and finally, he invited Daisy as he wished. On the day he met Daisy, he was embarrassed and uneasy like a young boy who is the first time to talk to the girl he loves. He arranged everything nervously, hoping to make a good impression on Daisy.
  • After Tom--the husband of Daisy-- knew the relationship between him and Daisy, his real identity was revealed by Tom. Daisy was shaken to leave with Gatsby. In the end, because of emotional instability, Daisy killed Mrs. Wilson, her husband's mistress, on the way home, and Gatsby was willing to commit the crime for him.
  • In the end, Mr. Wilson thought Gatsby had killed his wife, so he shot Gatsby at his home, and then committed suicide by himself. Only Nick and his father attend his funeral. At that time, Daisy and Tom move to another place.

Main Characters

Tom Buchanan is a man born from a wealthy family. Tom loves fitness, plays polo, and has a strong body. He gives Daisy material satisfaction and meets her vanity. Tom is lazy, unfaithful to his family, and loves to engage in extramarital affairs.

Gatsby is a man who is obsessed with love and his dreams. He feels low self-esteem because of his origin. He desires an amount of money to change his identity and status. He is personable and gentle who is the dream lover of many young girls.

Daisy comes from a wealthy family. She is beautiful and has a lively and interesting personality. There are many people fond of her. But in essence, she has the characteristics of high-class society selfishness, hypocrisy, and the superficiality of worshipping money.
